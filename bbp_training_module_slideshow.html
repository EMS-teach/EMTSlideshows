Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this module, you will be able to:

  • Define bloodborne pathogens and identify common types
  • Understand OSHA's Bloodborne Pathogens Standard (29 CFR 1910.1030)
  • Recognize modes of transmission and exposure risks in EMS
  • Apply proper personal protective equipment (PPE) protocols
  • Implement exposure control measures and post-exposure procedures
  • Follow proper hand hygiene and decontamination procedures
Note: This training fulfills one component of OSHA's Bloodborne Pathogens Standard requirements. You must also receive workplace-specific training from your employer.
Sources: OSHA 29 CFR 1910.1030, CDC Guidelines, AAOS EMT Emergency Care 12th ed., Nancy Caroline's Emergency Care in the Streets 9th ed., EMS Student Handbook (Camp Community College)

What Are Bloodborne Pathogens?

Definition: Bloodborne pathogens are pathogenic microorganisms present in human blood that can cause disease in humans.

Primary Bloodborne Pathogens of Concern

  • Hepatitis B Virus (HBV) - Attacks the liver, can cause chronic infection
  • Hepatitis C Virus (HCV) - Leading cause of liver transplants
  • Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) - Causes AIDS

Why EMS Personnel Are at Risk

  • Direct contact with blood and bodily fluids during patient care
  • Uncontrolled emergency environments
  • Potential for large volumes of blood at scenes
  • Use of sharp instruments (needles, scalpels)
  • Resuscitation procedures involving airways
